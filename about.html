About the ABRSM exam



Each ABRSM practical piano exam is split into four sections - pieces, scales & arpeggios, sight reading and finally aural skills.


You are marked out of 150. A pass is marked at 100, a merit at 120 and a distinction is 130 or above.


For each grade, students must prepare 3 pieces from a list of 30. These 30 pieces are divided into 3 lists (A, B and C). Each list contains 10 songs. Students must prepare 1 song from each list. This part of the exam is worth 90 marks or 60% of your total!


List A pieces are generally faster and require a more agile technique to play.


List B pieces are more lyrical and will challenge your expressivness while playing piano.


List C pieces are chosen to challenge you to play traditional and challenging styles.


If you have never stuidied piano before you can start at grade 1. The grades get progressively harder in order to motivate and challenge students at all ages and skill levels.
