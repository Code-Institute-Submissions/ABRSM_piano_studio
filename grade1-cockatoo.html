Cockatoo


Elissa Milne


This is a piece Elissa Milne wrote for her book "Very easy little peppers". It is a book of short jazz pieces for beginner pianists.


The song is meant to immitate the sound of a cockatoo, which is a type of parrot from Australia.


You can use the video to listen and follow along with the sheet music and to learn the correct fingerings.





How to play this song


Swing your quavers (8th notes)


This is a Jazz piece. Jazz quavers are played as if they are the first and last triplet instead of being played evenly. This is known as a swing 8th note. An 8th note is the same as a quaver.



Jazz Feel

Jazz music should be played slightly behind the beat. If that is confusing, then all you need to know for now is that you should "lay back" and relax when playing this piece!


Dynamics


Pay close attention to the crescendo and diminuendo in bars 3, 7 and 11. They are very important




Double Stops


Pay attention to all of the double stops in this piece. Play them together and make sure the sound is very clear in both notes.
