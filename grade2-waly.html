O Waly Waly (The water is wilde)


English Trad Song


This piece is taken from a collection of folk songs callef, folk roots for piano.


The origin and composer is uncertain but lyrics date back to the 1600's when the melody was set to a song called The water is wilde. In more recent years it has been recorded by artist such as Bob Dylan and Joan Baez.


You can use the video to listen and follow along with the sheet music and to learn the correct fingerings.





How to play this song


Right hand comping


This piece is special because the right hand does the comping. The left hand plays the melody. The right hand chords should be played lighter than usual so as not to overpower the left hand melody.


Right hand technique


Firstly, these are chords so make sure you are pressing all three notes down at the same time. Second, keep your fingers pressed down as long as possible to create a "sticky" effect.
