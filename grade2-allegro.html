Allegro


Thomas Attwood


Thomas Attwood was an English composer born in 1765. He travelled to Vienna and studied with Mozart before returning to England were he was employed as the music tutor of the royal family.


His composition style is very similar to Mozart. This piece is from a selection with the title of lessons which suggests it was written for teaching purposes.


You can use the video to listen and follow along with the sheet music and to learn the correct fingerings.





How to play this song


Left hand comping


The bass hand in this piece is quite busy. Make sure you don't accent the notes too loudly as to overpower the melody. It should have a floating, light and breezy feel.


Right hand phrasing

At the end of each phrase on your right hand, make sure to lift your hand up so as to give the phrase breadth.


The hard phrase


Bars 16 - 19 need special attention. The eight notes should all be even. The phrase should have a crescendo(get louder) and should also poco rit(get slower)
